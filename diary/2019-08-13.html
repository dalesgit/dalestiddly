The seven "Oh".

70 is a big number but it's not quite prime. Ah, there's more. It's a round number that isn't thereby hip. Looked at from below, it is a scary number. But quite tame from the vantage of, say, 75 or 80.

By age 70 one doesn't hold a lot of stock in birthdays, But your mother would scoff at thinking it old at all. The age one *is* and the age one *feels* are not the same. Just See me when I totter out of bed at night.

70 gives us lots of space to flow in and out, Like the clouds flowing past a hot-air balloon; Experience undergirding our presence, While wonder carries us deeper than Crater Lake.

Our children have reached an age that seemed out of reach When we were young. Wisdom remains as elusive as then. I would love to own the rhythm to dance the liturgy, But your arm in mine is sufficient for ever and always.

That hat I gave you as you gave me a flapper-smile, lit your face and smote my heart And never more shall I not love you or Stanch the pull of just to look you in the eyes.

What? you ask. Just to see you is my satisfaction. We make a dance to the melody of life, in a two-step shuffle Or just arm in arm, doing our best. "On the lips", you tell me, pointer finger on your mouth.
