Memories

So many memories:

  • visiting at the Museum of Science and Industry. Not in later years with my children, but when I was still a youth. The oversized chair. The dinosaur. The u-boat I wanted to tour but didn’t, then.
  • Playing caanasta with Peter into the wee hours. Or Monopoly in such a way that we could keep the game going on indefinitely.
  • The spook big trees at their old house. I was afraid they would blow down in the storms that were common in the summers.
  • Late the chance to see Suzanne’s breasts that I gave up. The smelling of pubic areas with Peter as still a boy. Later her breasts in innocence in the basement of 690 Dudley. Perhaps Peter’s predilections were a part of his early death?
  • Lydia asked for Pastor’s carrots recipe last night. I delivered today.
  • My own hard heartedness? How do I explain it? Understand it? But it’s very real.

Someday all the memories will just vanish. Like the files in this computer when they’re expunged. Quickly at the blink of an eye. When I don’t intend it.

Ancient the reflection that are lives are but the blinking of an eye. In today’s world we can more easily recognize that human existence – perhaps 1-2 million years? – is but the blinking of an eye.

What is all this knowledge for?

As a youth I wondered – literally had wonder about – the fact that I was a person who could think the thoughts that I was, and that if I were a young person living in, say, Zaire, as it was then known, or perhaps it was the Belgian Congo when I reflected such, that if I were that young man I would be thinking very different thoughts.

Oh, the wonder.
