Dinner with Peter & Christina {#Dinner with Peter & Christina}

Trying to talk heady stuff with Peter felt like it didn't go very far. He mostly was eager to talk about his work. My arguing from my perspective didn't seem to connect. I had too many parentheses in so that he didn't follow. I made reference to my concern that Elizabeth Schussler-Fiorenza was too often arguing from silence. Peter just looked at me not understanding and not following.

Cf. the principles of feminist criticism from the OT text:

  • looking at unexamined women's roles
  • looking for the censored and unspoken part of the text
  • changing the interpretative thrust of the text so that later generations can do better than the exploitation that past generations have done

Cf. also the text Sarah, Hagar, and their children where all the authors are feminist theologians: Christian, Jewish, & Muslim.

What is interesting is my seeming need to stretch my thinking, even as MP was feeling left out. All too often I am feeling the same way when she is controlling the conversation.
