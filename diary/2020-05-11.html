Teachers

  • 4th grade straight A's :: It only made an impression later in life when I learned that it had caused a major conflict with my sister who was also in the class.
  • Beulah Adams :: We made fun of her. She taught me an essential skill related to language.
  • High School History :: One short unit on "Shot heard round the world" and I learned that learning was not about facts and data but about inquiry.
  • Senior English (Honors) :: Learned that better grades came with using Cliff Notes and that learning came with struggling.
  • Harvey Rabin :: ethics & morality, foundation in Greek language
  • Joseph Pickle :: concern for me, mix pastoral with academic, Mr. God, my name is Anna, Mr. Blue, Diary ... of, Red & Black, (what else in that introductory class?) But then we read Kierkegaard together.
  • Piano teachers :: Language skill, trivium & quadrivium, 7 liberal arts. 'Before they became known by their Latin variations (artes liberales, septem artes liberales, studia liberalia),[3] the liberal arts were the continuation of Ancient Greek methods of enquiry that began with a "desire for a universal understanding."'
  • Tom Deeds :: colleague, but one I followed wherever he went
  • Terry Milligan :: like Tom, but my peer. He was interested in []{#continual learning}continual learning like me. cf. report 5/14. Terry was like a spiritual twin.
  • Louis Weil :: principle of oldest liturgical practice (name?). Laws against celibacy are a way of dating the practice.
  • Bart DeMerchant :: hi-lo Anglican vs. belief in a living God.
  • (NH Librarian) :: knowing only 4 things
  • Stanley Hauerwas :: brutal honesty
  • NH Ethics professor :: Kierkegaard and Buddhism, "crazy"?
  • Patrisha Wismer :: gave me a taste of teaching
  • John Dunne :: spirituality -- all is mysticism

Characteristics

  • telling me incisive things I remember
  • many I didn't connect very well with, but they gave me something that has fed me for a life: language, basic research tools,
  • Most of them I didn't think of as *friends*, but all as somehow "fellow citizens"
