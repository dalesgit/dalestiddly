The Rev. Dale Hathaway has now completed his 4th academic year as an adjunct professor in our department. He has established himself as a well respected professor whom colleagues and students regard as a person of integrity. He typically teaches three courses per semester, two sections of our Introduction to Religious Studies (RELG 101) and one other, which varies. This year, he had four sections of 101 (with a coupld of attached Honors sections) and Intro to Hebrew Bible / Old Testament (RELG 313) in fall and Modern Christian Thought (RELG 317) in spring.

I can say from observation of his office presence, discussion with him, and review of his course syllabi and other materials that Dale pours himself whole heartedly into his teaching. This is equally reflected now in his reporting on each of his courses and his reflections on student evaluations and student learning. For each course, he gives a review of what he aims to accomplish, the changes he has made to his materials and delivery, what worked well and what not so well, and the student reactions. Dale definitely cares about his students beyond their simply succeeding in his course, but their intellectual development, and their ability to read, think, and write critically and intelligently in our field of religions, religious studies, and theology.

Student evaluations are indeed generally quite positive. We must work, however, to find ways to improve student response to the online course evaluation. Of all six courses Dale taught, the response rates were 68%, 67%, 61%, 52%, 41%, and 38%. this has to be better.

It is also of some interest that in each of these sections, there are always students who express their dissatisfaction with the clarity of Dale's expectations. They are quite clearly a minority, but this may be something to work on in the future.

In his summary, Dale mentions having published some of his professional writings. As an adjunct, he is not required to maintain a scholarly profile, but it would be interesting for him to list some of his work in the Scholarly Activity section of the report so that we can have an appreciation of his writing and presentations.

Another very good year for Dale Hathaway. We are grateful to have him teaching on a regular basis for us, especially since a number of our full-timers are serving in administrative roles and so are not teaching their usual load. Dale is an asset to the department in both skill and attitude.
