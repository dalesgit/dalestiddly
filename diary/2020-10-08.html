The last piece

The first thing I meet in the kitchen upon waking up, maneuvering to make the coffee for Mary Pat, is the pie pan in the sink. Thrown in the sink as a catch all. Sean ate the last piece of the French Silk Pie. Some time after we went to bed, on his own time, not with us.

And the focus of my bitterness toward is focused on that piece of pie. I see it as arrogance. Take whatever you can get away with. Sugar, sugar. No respect. Act like a 10 year old.

The fact that Mary Pat felt like she had to work so hard. The fact that I had told her several times that it was the best French Silk Pie – last night I told her it was the best since my mother.

I know it’s narrow-minded of me. But I feel it deep in my craw. The cost of him being here is so great. He doesn’t know. #discernment # Charlotte 10/8/20
