Amazingly, earlier in the day the TV in the living room was showing a special that Katy had recorded on the natural history of butterflies. The photography was breathtaking. The story even more so. There is a butterfly that migrates twice as far as monarch butterflies, from Africa, across the Sahara and Mediterranean up to northern Europe. Scientists had long known that they disappeared there before re-emerging in Africa. Only recently apparently have scientists discovered that at the end of their journey they soar high into the air an capture strong wind currents that carry them back in a matter of days to their place of origins.
