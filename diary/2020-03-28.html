The crack in bowl? {#The crack in bowl?}

Perhaps it is the issue of wanting and choosing. I spent a lot of my life doing what I "ought" to do, thus sacrificing who I was. Pleasing my father, etc. But "choosing" something and being okay with it seems equally powerful to me. It feels like all of the great acts of generosity and sacrifice are based on that. Jäggerstätter, Jesus, Henry Nouwen. Each of them found their joy in doing what they chose. Maybe that's it. Finding my joy.

Ross Gay was claiming "delight" for his life. From the movie last night, Good Will Hunting, we saw a heart-warming love story -- heart-breaking too. Will chose in the end to see what would happen with "a girl". He didn't know the answer but he accepted the concept that he had to be all in to be able to find out. I thought repeatedly last night that that's what I had done with Mary Pat.
