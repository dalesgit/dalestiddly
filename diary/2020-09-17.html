It’s not fair! Kingdom of Heaven: Like a landowner … I ended with being struck by the way in which I need to live my days, one day at a time.

Kerr, landowner gave them a job, so they had an assignment from the get go. Something to be grateful for.

Each day a day to be grateful for. The students were fabulous.

In the end the day collapsed

MP wanted to go to bed early. I think she was angry at me. Don’t know why. Maybe she didn’t think I stirred the soup appropriately. Maybe she wanted me to volunteer to go get Sean because she repeated several times that she was “so tired.”

After supper we watched the show with a Syrian refugee doctor. Then she announced that she was going to bed, asking me what I was going to do. I said I’d be in shortly. Sean announced that he was going to watch some show he wanted to see. I announced that I would be leaving the living room, they both knew that it was in disgust.

I came to bed and almost finished the book Why? the Holocaust? Depressing stuff. And unbending and principled in a way, but overthrowing some assumptions of the modern world, but leaving things at a heavy existentialist sort of decision-point.

That’s what I feel. I don’t think the marriage is going to make it. Because MP will always choose her son over me. And I don’t think I can make it until he leaves. I can’t abide his attitude of privilege and entitlement … (deservingness)? which is fostered by what I think is correctly named by Linda MP’s codependency.
