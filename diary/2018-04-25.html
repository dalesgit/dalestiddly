Mom is going to die in the next days or at most weeks. What do I want to record for my feelings here?

Is it sadness? We've just watched Revolutionary Road with Leonardo DiCaprio, Kate Winslett, Kathy Bates et. al.Â It was a rendition of the ennui that kills quintessentially presented in the 1950's. I saw my mother writ all over the film. She wanted adventure. She wanted to do the crazy thing. She tried the crazy sexual adventures. But she settled. Didn't she settle?

And the adopted first child that none of us knew about. Wasn't that part of it? Of course. But now it's over. And what do I feel.

I feel tired. I'm 67 and outlived my father. I have a bunch of children from whom I am estranged. I have a woman who married me for love and loves me still. We pray together daily. I have been struggling to find a voice in my sermons and some people have resonated with them.

I gave the young couple who want to get married a copy of my blogs, urging them to read the Anglican presentation. Peter and Kristina want to come to Our Savior this coming Sunday because I'm going to be celebrating. I don't know how to live up to the expectations.

And yet, I think I need to go on, "right now, to celebrate another day. I'll go on."
