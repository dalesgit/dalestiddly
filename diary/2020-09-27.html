Morning

20201001220943 Sleep ok. Dreams don’t hang onto me like a grown up bully or a toddler weight. I’m aware of feeling disgust at the presence of Sean’s naked body slouched in my living room. I’m aware of judgment and projection with the awareness of his brooding smoking right outside my door. I’m reminded of Billy’s presence at mom’s house on Venus Dr. I rehearse the line, “I didn’t sign up for this. This is not how I want to live my last years.” But I then quickly respond to myself, “That’s the human condition. We don’t get what we expect or want. We end up walking on 3 legs and somebody else taking care of us.”

So I imagine my lot is to learn to put up with someone’s constant presence whom I don’t like or approve of. More than that I don’t trust him. I expect him to betray me. But that’s what I’ve got and will have.

The big looming test I put before MP – she couldn’t respond – was going to Dayton for funeral – or whatever. She’s looking to him having a license so he can drive himself to work. Even then, I told her, I don’t trust Sean living in this house by himself.

But it’s not my call.

I do think of going to Olympia – or at least the west coast – for a while. David company. Miriam and kids.
