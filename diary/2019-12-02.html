Struggle with MP {#Struggle with MP}

Why do you stay with me? she asked, expressing the notion that she has never been good for me.

  1. because you have the eloquence and self-awareness to write the letter to your family
  2. because you express you love through cooking -- thinking of the amount of work that went into Thanksgiving Day.
  3. I thought about the standing on one leg she did, like me, as a youth -- but I wanted current things
  4. her commitment to trying to live her life in accordance with her faith, even in the midst of continually falling short
  5. her ability with numbers and finance
  6. her deep commitment to support the little guy, the underdog, the disadvantaged

In dreaming and dozing I was aware of the double-binds she gives me. She seems to not listen well -- or her mind jumps too fast to her response or the thing to []{#fix it.}fix it. She doesn't accept my judgments, treating them perhaps as pronouncements and then ignoring them. The most prominent example is that I have repeatedly asked that there be a system of "allowance" so we each had money that is just ours to spend or save as we see fit. She ignores it because that's basically what she has with all the money.

It seemed important to me to point to the ways that we experience the world differently and that it leads to lots of our disagreements. I gave the example of calendars:

  • she thinks of calendars as written in stone
  • she puts to do items on the calendar
  • I think of calendars as "targets", or "possibilities" that can easily be changed
  • there is, of course, the need for space versus the need for someone to talk to

But the important part was that she didn't seem to hear me, or it didn't register, or she didn't see any significance to it, or she had such a need to tell me what she was thinking and feeling. I guess I give off a sense of not hearing her. So often I get the sense that she thinks I'm a very shallow person, judging by the way she responds to me.

All of these are insignificant in the larger scheme of things. I told her that I wasn't going to leave her -- except for death. She didn't want to go there with me, perhaps because death is all over her consciousness right now.

Later in the morning, on her way out the door, I asked what was going on in her head. Her answers made me feel like, again or still, it's all my fault and my problem to solve. I showed her the text from Jim Croom that seems to say that his surgery schedule will be 1 week later than he had hoped in his last communication. I've tried to stay current with MP with what I know. Clearly her expectations are dramatically different from mine.

She says:

  1. She still doesn't know if there is going to be any time for her to go Dayton.
  2. She still doesn't know if I will talk to her sisters when I get to Dayton. There is an echo of the fact that when she put me on speaker on Thanksgiving Day, that I had nothing to say to her gaily talking sisters, speaking as if nothing was wrong.
