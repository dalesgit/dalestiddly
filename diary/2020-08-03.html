Sue Monk Kidd’s journey

I have just finished her memoir of the 90’s, When the heart waits. I ended it, leafing through the notes, thinking, “This is an account of me. If I had been a writer, this is what I would have written. Certainly these are the authors I was reading and the writers who impacted me the most.”

Yesterday I finished her latest novel, The Book of Longings, imagining the wife of Jesus, representing the divine feminine. Again, I recognized the importance of the Nag Hammadi texts in her writing the novel, the feminine voice, “Little Thunder” the epithet Jesus used of her, and the text from the collection of “The Thunder Perfect Mind”:

I am the first and the last
I am she who is honored and she who is mocked
I am the whore and the holy woman…

Now I am looking at beginning the earlier memoir, about discovering God’s love, God’s joyful surprise. Looking at the notes, here too I will find much that is familiar by way of nostalgia. “If I had been a writer …”. But there is no point in turning away from the sacred now.
