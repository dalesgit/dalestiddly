The ease with which I can get a new note started is pretty cool. I am almost at the end of Caring for Words and I don’t want it to end. The author’s so-evident delight with words connects with me in a visceral and nostalgic place, taking me back to the days I memorized huge swaths of poetry, was passionate about George Steiner, was in-spired by Eliot and Yeats, was enthralled with the imaginative force as well as the message of Kierkegaard, thought language must be the key to what it meant to be human. After an all-too-ordinary 5 decades on, I can see that much of that, while sophomoric infatuation, provided the foundation for much of the rest of my life.

I have sometimes shared with conversation partners the sense of being overwhelmed with responsibility when I realized in Wauwatosa that the rest of my life would be dependent on the quality of my word craft. I’ve been better than some but mostly just ordinary. 

Listening to Mary Chapin Carpenter’s 10,000 miles. Sweet music. I think it’s folk-like that is my favorite music. I could listen to that.
