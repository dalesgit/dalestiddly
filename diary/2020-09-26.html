Waking early

The mind has been racing to put words together to form sentences. Sean this. Because of Sean that. I think of Scott and his weird hours for sleeping. And I think, “So, it has begun.” My dotage.

MP had asked me what I expected. It allowed me to say that I had expected him to get a bicycle and with the bus system to explore and have access to all of Rock Hill. Instead he has stayed holed up in this house, utterly dependent on his mother for transportation.

Not surprisingly she has step by step allowed him to become her solar system. I saw early how Sean’s appetites were ravenous for things he liked or craved. Sugar. Carbohydrates. Meat. I would say that it’s just fine with him to have his mother be his sun and moon while he gets his parole behind him. “Normal life?” What would that be for him.

But my point the other day was that there is no end in sight for Sean’s presence. And for me it feels ugly and it leaves me out in the cold. I guess, now, writing it down, that I might well be advised to really begin to put into place my universe. I had so thoroughly moved in to live in a universe of 2 some 9 years ago. I let go of much. I shall need to reclaim that. She can have her universe around her son, but I ought not to lose myself as compensation.

That is, I think, where the session with Linda was headed. Perhaps the therapeutic perspective goes there as well.

So I accept it and plan on shopping for myself I guess. Or try to piece myself into her puzzle. She wants me to be a 3rd wheel. I don’t want that.
