Poem for Mary Pat {#Poem for Mary Pat}

Birthday on the beach, Picnic at Magic Island. A paddler surfs slow.

Dream of haiku days Lazily shuffle seaward Watch the lit lanterns.

69 begins In the hearth of family, Your focus on Mom

Tree perched summer books Your mind and heart devoured them This summer again.

Turning sixty-nine It starts like a sly wry wink, A sere winsome smile.

Cultivating wine, The grace to metabolize well- Aged to perfection.

Today a moment, This is not an estimate, But a precise now.
