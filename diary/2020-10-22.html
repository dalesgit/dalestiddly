Precipitates and elephants

Thu Oct 22, 2020 05:27 AM

Yesterday I had a whole new level of understanding the gulf between us. I really didn’t appreciate it adequately. Mary Pat was retaliating, I think, against the time a day or two previous when she was casually referring to some way in which our calendar that day was determined by Sean’s schedule, and I cut off conversation by saying, “That’s a Sean conversation that we can’t pursue right now.”

So we were in the bathroom. I was grooming somehow. She was getting out of the WC. She made some kind of comment about not liking her hair. I made what was intended as a light-hearted comment, thinking of the many women, mostly women of color, who have shaved their heads – often but not always in support of breast cancer solidarity – I said, “you could shave your head.”

She responded by gathering a very stern face and walking away, saying something to the effect, “The word ‘shave’ was the occasion for an major argument we had; so we can’t have that conversation.”

What she was recalling was a time that also caught me by surprise. Again she was talking about her hair and she made a reference to “shaving her head”, meaning the way in which her old hair person would cut one side of her head short, several times very short. She had probably used an electric shaver to cut part of her head, rather than scissors. MP and I were hearing the word “shave” to mean two different things. She understood, I think, using the tool to cut it very short. I understood “shave” to mean what Sean and Owen do to their head.

MP got extremely angry with me when I said, “She didn’t actually ‘shave’ your head.” MP said very angrily, “I was there. I know what she was doing to me.” At the time, either explicitly or via my intuition, I thought that she was projecting Doug, estranged and bullying husband, onto me. It wasn’t the first time I felt that, and not just when she confused our names, “Dale” “Doug”. “Shave” became a “trigger”, a “catalyst”, a “precipitate”.

As I learned yesterday, “shave” was yet one more subject we couldn’t talk about. One thing I have come to appreciate more fully since my last visit in Charlotte, is that the alienation between MP and me is greater than I understood. The things we “can’t talk about” have grown and grown.
