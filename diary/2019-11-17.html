Goodbyes to Gini {#Goodbyes to Gini}

For me it was short, but I felt like crying. I said, "Good night, Gini." She responded by saying, "You'll be back tomorrow won't you?" I said, "No, we're going home for a while." She said, "Oh, drive safely." Then she said, "I'll miss you."

Three sisters hugged me, each trying to convey something through the hug. I thought afterwards that I had felt more honesty and genuine friendship from cousins that I hardly knew while traveling in Florida.

I felt throughout the 7 hours at David Street today that I was disconnected from everybody. That I had nothing to say to anyone. I felt cold. Sterilized. Impotent. Ineffective.

It feels like no one pays particular attention to me. I'm know it's not that no one cares, least of all MP. But I am cut off and my thoughts are ignored, no, rather just not heard. To strangers, Maria in the breakfast room, clerks at Target or Meier, to them I can respond with warmth and playfulness, wit and kindness, and I feel alive.

Part of me feels dead inside.
