I told him about:

  • Scott telling me about "FINE". Sean observing the significance of acronyms referred to one that evoked fight or flight impulse. It was FEAR: fuck everything and run.
  • Scott telling me that he had survived one relapse but he couldn't survive another
  • Hearing 2 voices: 1 my father telling me that he was OK and it was going to be okay. The other was in Sacred Heart Chapel (Basilica) when not knowing what to do I heard: "I don't care what you do as long as you love me with your whole heart, your soul, your body, and your mind." (or was it "strength" -- I've forgotten.)
  • the prisoner at Michigan Penitentiary -- I would entrust my two girls to him. The most adverse circumstances, when responded to with the right attitude and behaviors, produces an astonishing transformation.
  • he talked about how the therapists had taught him about cognitive therapy. That there was event, emotions and feelings, action. That if you want a different result from your past you have to interfere with that sequence.
  • The conversation began with passing a church named "Shekinah". I explained it meant the "presence" of God. I asked him if he had experienced something like that. He thought for a moment and then said that one time was his decision to go to Halawa. That the pieces he could identify argued that going to a treatment center was the best immediate choice. But he somehow knew that the difficult decision was the best one for him. God's voice telling him.
