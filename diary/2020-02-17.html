  • I'm sure you are aware that you didn't do well on your exam. This result doesn't have to be a disaster for your grade. It can very well be a wake-up call to success. Please come to see me during my office hours or contact me to make an appointment for another time. I would like to talk with you about what you can do to succeed in the future in our class.
  • One key to success is to recognize that if you do something in the essay questions you will get credit. Most of the poor results were associated with exams with no response to a question.   • One surprise for me was the generally poor performance on the vocabulary items. Some missed zero. But many missed 8, 9, 10 or more. Again, you knew a week in advance the items that would be on the exam. I told you where to find the answers in two books.
  • Review the rubrics for exam. Illustrate the 4 point checklist.
  • Method was to average 5 grades: 1 for vocabulary, 4 essays.
  • Basic importance: I am not looking for a reflection on "what you think or feel about a subject." I am looking for evidence that you have been listening, that you read, that you have been thinking about the material. An example is: we have repeated over and over that religion does not necessarily have to do with God, church, worship -- though that may be your experience of it. Religion has been defined for us as having to do with the sacred and how it works in human lives and how it is manifest in human lives.
