& the Art of Motorcycle Maintenance =

It is late. Time to have been in bed for an hour, but I'm not. I am getting to know "Phaedrus". He is the alter-ego of the narrator. He is the person the narrator had been before he went insane. He is a thinker. He struggles to avoid the nihilism which is thought seems to thrust him. He understands the conversations about "a priori" and other extended metaphors. I read them now and remember back to the resonance they had for me when I was 20 y/o.

Dreaming

There was a kind of normalcy to my dreams. I can't actually remember them now. I have worked for a while. But they weren't disturbing although fantastic in a dream sort of way. Helped perhaps by reading the book before turning out the lights. I was able to recognize, or at least remember a little bit, my very young self as a 20 year-old, caught up in the reveries of the novel and its antagonist? Phaedrus.
