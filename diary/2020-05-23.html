Open source to Riley {#Open source to Riley}

Open-source is probably the biggest factor. By that I don't mean "free". Goes back to the line that there's free as in beer -- and free as in speech. Open-source software is free speech. A good case can be made that the world runs on open-source software -- even though it is closed-source outfits that have monopolized and made money. But it ends up flexible. Lots of options. In some cases there is more opportunity than in closed-source. Other times one has to settle for "a little more awkward." But I would say that everything you can do with closed-source you can do better in open-source. But you may have to work a little harder. An example is that I am currently taking a certification course on Blackboard so that Winthrop University will let me teach online -- even though I have been doing it since 2011. The fact is that because of the work I have done in open-source software I can make Blackboard do things that most people can't imagine. Another goes back 15 years or so. I used to use a program called Leo. It was a versatile program for managing information. There was a glitch that I couldn't solve in an export I was trying to accomplish.
