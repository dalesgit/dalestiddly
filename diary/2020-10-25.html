Dream of Dad

I’m writing, having just awakened enough to manage this. I was leading a group that felt mostly like Monroe Episcopalians. Some elements of Hawai’i and my traditional dream where I was having trouble doing what was supposed to be done. The church had somehow suffered some devastation, flooding? But with a lot of help from folks I started the prayer. It was an introduction. Whoever was usually in charge – a female bishop? the normal pastor? – had told me that while she was doing a more familiar rite 2 something, I was to do Rite 1 Morning Prayer. I had to think about it. WHile I was thinking, I did this introduction. I did that sort of thing in Monroe. They liked. I enjoyed myself.

The focus was prayer. I commended them in their prayer. People had suffered, perhaps died, so I knew they were grieving and I knew that there were faithful prayers. But I started with the example that Mary Pat prayed. I knew about that. And I knew second hand that they were prayers – I was visualizing (the Sr. Warden and her husband? I can’t say the names right now.)

But then I looked up and within about 15 feet, it felt like right in front of me, was my father, looking right at me.

I was overcome, like the speaker on the news giving reports on Covid, I had trouble going on and I felt a flood of tears coming over me.

I did keep going, but only briefly before I woke.

Major insight?

Really in the shower, but after a nap, trying to think of things to do to escape the doldrums I feel, I unexpectedly realized that the satisfaction that I get from solving computer puzzles, or of getting a computer to “do something” is kin to the satisfaction I get from woodworking and sewing. Julian and I have it.

Maybe I will start cooking like soups or gaspacho like stuff. Healthy eating.
