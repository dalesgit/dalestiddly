Journaling

The counselor sent me material about journaling. I haven’t looked at it yet. But, late, after Mary Pat has gone and fetched Sean and I have retreated into the bedroom, I begin reading Sue Monk Kidd. It is her journal from when she was maybe in her 30’s. The beginning of her awakening. At the beginning she announces she wants to become a writer. She tries to divide her life into spheres and apportion out her responsibilities in each. I thought, “What she is going through is analogous to what I am going through with Sean.” He is like a child added to the family. My challenge is for finding my own space.

The t-shirt Mary Pat gave me says, in effect, that I have to find time for playing piano. Writing, too.
