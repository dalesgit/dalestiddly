Holy week over: Easter {#Holy week over: Easter}

People crying {#People crying}

First there was the wife of the junior warden. Tears streaming down on, was it Palm Sunday? I went and tried to give her words that bring God to bear.

Then Mary Pat reported the mother of the 2 teen age girls. She wept uncontrollably after my Vigil sermon. She said to Mary Pat that she often heard voices that said bad things. And that while I was preaching I cast out the voices. The next day she gave almost a mantra: "Extravagance and Abundance."

Jo S. said that my time with them had made the frozen church a little less frozen.

Henry said, "I'm going to miss you." Every sermon you give me something to think about.

There were many others. And then I felt a little bit hurt that I learned that the new priest would be there on July 8 at the same time the rest of the congregation learned that detail. Still none of us knows the name of this person. We know he's a he.

Judges

The meal was breathtaking. antipasto plate. rare steaks with shrimp scampi with scallops. a sort of German potato salad. beans that were steamed then sauteed in various things. cheese plater. some kind of cheese frittata sort of thing with Gran Marnier sprinkled on top.

But most amazing was that we seemed to go deep pretty easily. Christina spoke of words Peter's brother had said to their daughter -- words that still burn. And she asked, "How to forgive." I tried to bring God's touch into place.
