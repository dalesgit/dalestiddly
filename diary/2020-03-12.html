Covid-19 and the church {#Covid-19 and the church}

Sam Rodman has produced a policy yesterday for the diocese that is pretty far-reaching. The only troubling part I felt had to do with his call that the clergy not participate in "common cup" as well as laity. I didn't know if he meant there would be no chalice on the altar. And if so how was the consecrated wine to be handled?

  • Consecrated elements should be handled reverently. I still remember Bart DeMerchant's story about seeing an altar guild somewhere in the country taking a loaf of consecrated bread and dumping it in the trash.
  • A principle for me since seminary has been that I don't pretend at anything during the liturgy. If there's no water in the cruet -- don't pretend there is. If there's no bread and wine -- you go get some.

For now Charlotte is ok with consuming the small amount of wine in chalice as I revest the chalice and paten. If we move to removing it to the sacristy I want there to be safe-guards that make sure it is handled and consumed reverently.

She is going to take care of notices to the congregation and seeing about the possibility of a notice in the bulletin.

She reports that the ongoing saga of the funeral has been quite something and that I should be glad I couldn't do it.
