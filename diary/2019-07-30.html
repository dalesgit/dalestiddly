The Navigator {#The Navigator}

I finished reading this novel yesterday. I really want to reflect in some way on the experience, because it seemed to touch me so deeply the 2 times I read it. I read it one time before going to Hawai'i and one time after leaving Hawai'i. Today in conversation with Sue Parker (3-way call) Mary Pat made reference to "being called" to Hawai'i.

A quick scan of the things I marked:

  • mana, passed from grandfather to grandson
  • geography and environments that sound familiar after living in Hawai'i
  • "Don't kid yourself ... we're all prisoners, of our genes, of our history, of our long, ancestral dreams ..."
  • the sensitivity to spiritual authority being exercised by a flawed human being
  • mana being something like the power that the Israelites knew was in the Ark of the Covenant, was with Moses when he went up on the mountain -- the people in the novel knew of this
  • the combination of Magnusson going to the place of the elders to die, and the same trip was the initiation rite that made Mark an adult, where he heard God or gods speak, "the voice"
  • pp 127-8, a priest (Flanagan) describes faith as the "arithmetic of the cosmos" providing order in chaos
  • p 156-7 for Gunnar there was not one cosmogony but many
  • p. 167 "The journey is more important than the arrival"
  • p. 200 "the essence of kapu ... the foundation of respect for established order."
  • p. 236 "All the dead look alike, ... the same flowers grow over them all. Problem is, a man's last hours belong only to him. No one has the right to intrude with complaints or excuses or fears or hates."
  • the controversies, dynamics of rise and fall, of the community
  • p 288 awareness of the human compulsion to mess things up
