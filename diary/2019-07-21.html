Peter and Christina's date was a happy occasion. "Chris's sister in law" was doing all the housework, kitchen-work etc. Beth Costner refused to say a word to Mary Pat. For me, perhaps, the most unexpected things were 2. The husband of Jean, the sociology chair, attends Our Saviour. He has reported to Jean that "this guy on your floor" preaches there and "he's really good." I may have blunted his good intentions by telling a story including how many people just do a perfunctory "I enjoyed your sermon father."

Secondly, I had occasion to talk to 2 students who knew me: Grant & Victoria Burdett. I talked with both about my struggle to discern whether I had a voice or a message that would be helpful to the students. Both of them were adamant that I did. Grant, whom I had for 3 classes, particularly was full of praise and encouragement. Earlier in the day, in my cleaning, I had run across a review sheet from Peter about my teaching. In a table of grades, he had marked all of them "acceptable" for an adjunct, except the top one about being prepared and knowledgable which he marked "superior".

Peter is working at home on 2 things.

  1. His proposed book on suffering. I shared my sermon where Amos informed the interpretation of the Mary/Martha pericope. I suggested listening for what the prophets had to say about suffering after he had expressed frustration at not finding a focus. He began with a quick overview -- which I don't have at my fingertips the way he does:
    • Job
    • accounts of suffering in the psalms
    • Isaiah, "suffering servant."
  2. His course on "Women in the Bible." He expressed surprise at how much he didn't know. I shared that Schussler-Fiorenza had been at Notre Dame when I was there and that I, too, had felt challenged by the femininist critique.

I envied him his theological skills and opportunities to exercise them.
