08/15/2020

71

Something in your voice that made me hear it.
Silence. “How does it feel to be 71?” Pause?

71

Like a classic car.
Like a Ham Radio code.
Like a ratio for vinaigrette.

Something in the way you move
That seems to leave this troubled world behind.

It’s measured but quantum, not Newtonian,
Musical-like, rhythm and tune. It’s the pause that gives the meaning,
Pirouette from plodding to soaring,
From melodrama to weightless grace Like the dancer’s leap, caught in mid-air.

She’s been with me now quite a long, long time
And I feel fine

It’s measured in breathless moments that rest between
The uttered and the utteree
Measured in the space between,
That binds, engages, encompasses, embraces, encircles, twines.

It isn’t what she’s got to say
But how she thinks and where she’s been

71 is a good vintage,

But 49 was the birth afterall,
That gives today our
Vintage wine. An anniversary to keep. Years to match.

And I feel fine anytime she’s around me now

Something in your voice, your eyes,
Your hand to the stars,
That brings rejoice in me.

Something I can’t say, you can’t hold it still,
Neither of us engrave, neither embalm,
Shrouded in the unspoken yearning
That spills onto the kitchen floor you’re
Dancing on to express your love
That makes me

feel fine.

49. 71. Today. The best of days.

It is good, he said. And on the 7th day rested,
Blessed to have come this far
With your hand in mine
And our silences in touch.

Thank you, my dear.
